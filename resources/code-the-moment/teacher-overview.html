Teacher Overview

Use this overview to understand the project before introducing it to students.

CodeBronx
Code the Moment

What students will make

Students choose one small moment from a story and turn it into a short Scratch animation using characters, setting, dialogue, movement, sequence, and debugging.

Best classroom fit

Use during narrative writing, character study, retelling, dialogue, sequence of events, point of view, or creative response to text.

CategoryRecommendation
Grade BandGrades 3 to 5. Adaptable for grades 2 to 6.
Time Needed45 minutes for a quick activity, 2 class periods for a mini-project, or 3 class periods for a full build and share cycle.
ToolScratch. Students may work individually or in pairs.
Final ArtifactA short animated scene that communicates one important story moment.
ELA FocusCharacter, setting, dialogue, action, sequence, theme, point of view, retelling, or narrative development.
CS/CT FocusDecomposition, sequencing, events, algorithms, debugging, and optional loops or costume changes.
Teacher reminder: Students should not code the entire story. A strong one-scene project is better than an unfinished whole-story project.
