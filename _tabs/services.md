# CodeBronx Services

CodeBronx helps schools and organizations make computing education practical. The work is designed for real classrooms, real teacher schedules, and real implementation constraints.

The focus is simple: help schools integrate computational thinking, AI literacy, and computer science into instruction without creating another disconnected initiative.

---

## What CodeBronx Helps Schools Build

### 1. Computational Thinking Integration

For schools that want to strengthen ELA, Math, STEM, or project-based learning through structured thinking routines.

**This can include:**

- ELA lesson extensions using decomposition, pattern recognition, abstraction, and algorithmic thinking
- Math tasks that connect reasoning, procedures, debugging, and problem solving
- Teacher-facing planning templates
- Student-facing graphic organizers, checklists, and reflection tools
- Curriculum review to identify natural computing connections

**Best for:** principals, instructional coaches, curriculum teams, and teachers who want CS/CT integration without adding a new class period.

---

### 2. AI Literacy for Schools

For schools that know AI is here but need a responsible, age-appropriate, instructionally useful approach.

**This can include:**

- AI literacy routines for students
- Teacher planning workflows using AI responsibly
- Student reflection and verification protocols
- Prompting as a thinking process, not a shortcut
- School-facing guidance for ethical and practical AI use

**Best for:** schools and districts trying to move beyond hype and build actual AI readiness.

---

### 3. Professional Development

Practical professional learning for educators who need usable strategies, not theory-heavy sessions that disappear after the workshop.

**Possible PD topics:**

- Integrating Computational Thinking Into ELA
- Integrating Computational Thinking Into Math
- AI Literacy for K–8 Classrooms
- Teaching Students to Think Before Using AI
- Computational Thinking as a Foundation for AI Readiness
- Robotics, Physical Computing, and Student Problem Solving
- Designing Classroom-Ready CS/CT Lesson Extensions

**Formats can include:**

- 60-minute introduction sessions
- 90-minute interactive workshops
- Half-day or full-day professional development
- Multi-session PD series
- Virtual or in-person facilitation

---

### 4. Coaching and Classroom Implementation

For schools that want support after the PD session ends.

**This can include:**

- Lesson planning support
- Model lessons
- Co-planning with teachers
- Classroom implementation feedback
- Student work review
- Refinement cycles based on what actually happens in the classroom

**Best for:** schools that want implementation, not just exposure.

---

### 5. STEM, Robotics, and Physical Computing Support

For schools and organizations building hands-on computing experiences through robotics, devices, design challenges, or project-based learning.

**This can include:**

- Robotics curriculum planning
- Physical computing activities
- Student design challenges
- Teacher training for robotics or computing tools
- Showcase and student presentation support
- Connections between robotics, literacy, problem solving, and career readiness

---

## What Schools Receive

Depending on the project, CodeBronx support may include:

- Professional development sessions
- Teacher-ready lesson materials
- Student-facing worksheets or planning tools
- Implementation guides
- Coaching cycles
- Model lesson support
- Curriculum maps

## The CodeBronx Approach

1. Start with the school’s current curriculum and goals
2. Identify where computing education naturally fits
3. Build practical teacher-facing tools
4. Support implementation through PD, coaching, or modeling
5. Refine based on teacher feedback and student work
